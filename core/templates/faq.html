Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers for your business Zakat compliance.

Zakatable assets are those that are "productive" or liquid, such as cash, gold, silver, and inventory intended for sale. Non-Zakatable assets include items for use, such as factory machinery, office buildings, delivery trucks, and furniture.
According to the majority of scholars, strong debts (money you are confident will be paid) should be included in your Zakatable total. Weak debts (bad debts you expect to lose) can be excluded until they are actually received.
Zakat is calculated on your "Zakatable Pool" (Cash + Inventory + Receivables) minus your immediate/short-term business liabilities. It is not a tax on your total revenue, but on your net surplus wealth held for one lunar year.
Nisab is usually calculated based on the value of 85 grams of gold or 595 grams of silver. ZakatTech uses a conservative gold-based threshold (approx. $6,450) to ensure compliance, though users should check local current rates for extreme precision.
Generally, only the upcoming year's installments (short-term portion) of a long-term loan are deductible from your Zakatable assets. The entire capital of a multi-year loan is typically not deducted all at once.
